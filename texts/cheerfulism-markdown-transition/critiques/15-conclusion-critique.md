# Critique: Chapter 15 — Conclusion

## Pacing and Structure

Nine lines. Three beats: real name reveal and personal statement, invitation to the reader, sign-off under the pen name. Clean arc. The real name reveal (Carlos Alegre Urquizu) followed by the pen name sign-off (Charles D. Cheerful) creates a deliberate doubling — both faces shown. The brevity is right. After everything that came before, a long conclusion would feel like backtracking.

## Claims and Explanations

"I just want to party with happier people" compresses the entire book's thesis into one conversational line. It works. The commitment to teach these ideas to his children is a strong implicit claim of confidence — the author is staking his parenting on the framework, which says more than any argument could.

## Overall

"I just want to party with happier people" is a genuinely good line. The brevity works. The name reveal gives it weight. No notes.
