# THE DEMOCRATIC CONSTITUTION

**Part 0: Introduction for the reader**

---

What would a democratic constitution look like if it were agnostic to any historical or cultural context? What would be a valid set of rules that responds as purely and objectively as possible to the fundamental power dynamics of a democratic system? Power is not a matter of culture or history; it is a matter of human nature. There should exist a set of constitutional rules applicable to any human society.

This book is an attempt to answer that question. One, out of intellectual curiosity. And two, to create a baseline against which any present and future society can compare its own constitution and reform it, incorporating more democratic dynamics into it.

## What you will find

**Part 1** is the pure constitutional text: articles, thresholds, mechanisms, procedures. No explanations, no justifications, no philosophy. Just the rules.

**Part 2** is the argumentation: why each design decision is the way it is. Every numerical threshold, every mechanism, every prohibition has a reason. If something seems arbitrary, excessive, or lenient in Part 1, the answer is in Part 2.

## Disclaimer

This constitution is unconventional in several ways. Some provisions will feel uncomfortable or strange.

The general approach is that of a security audit: with the mindset of a hacker, every mechanism is subjected to the question "how does this break?". It is assumed that someone with unlimited resources will attempt to capture the system, and it is designed so that the cost of doing so is prohibitive. Good will on the part of rulers is not assumed. No one's morality is trusted. Trust is placed in the mechanisms, which although always imperfect as in any human system, are robust enough to minimize the probability of a breach.

## Disclaimer regarding the review

This text has been reviewed article by article, searching for ambiguities, inconsistencies, broken references, and attack vectors. Dozens of findings have been corrected during that process. Even so, a constitution is a complex system and there may always be something left to polish. If you find something that does not fit, feel free to contact the author.

## How to read this book

It is recommended to read Part 1 first, from beginning to end, without dwelling on details that may seem strange. Then, read Part 2 to understand the reasoning. Many decisions that appear arbitrary in Part 1 make sense in Part 2.
