# TITLE I: FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

### Article 1: Popular Sovereignty and Hierarchy of Legitimacy

Sovereignty resides in the people. The people exercise their sovereignty through the direct and indirect mechanisms established in this Constitution.

The following hierarchy of democratic legitimacy is established, from highest to lowest:

1. Popular Power, which is exercised through the direct election of representatives (articles 10 and 13), the recall of legislative representatives (articles 11 and 12), the popular annulment of legislative decisions (article 6) and judicial decisions (article 46), and the initiative and referendum for constitutional reform (articles 67 through 69 inclusive).
2. Delegated powers: Legislative (Subtitle 1 of Title IV), Executive (Subtitle 2 of Title IV), and Judicial (Subtitle 3 of Title IV), equal among themselves and subject to reciprocal oversight in accordance with the mechanisms established in this Constitution (Subtitles 4-9 of Title IV).

Popular Power may oppose the decisions of any delegated power solely in accordance with the procedures established in this Constitution. No delegated power prevails over another; their conflicts are resolved through the mutual oversight mechanisms that this Constitution establishes.

---

### Article 2: Consensus Levels

This Constitution establishes six consensus levels for decision-making:

1. Level N1: 51%.
2. Level N2: 60%.
3. Level N3: 66%.
4. Level N4: 75%.
5. Level N5: 85%.
6. Level N6: 95%.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, percentages are calculated on the total number of valid votes cast. When an article specifies "of the electoral roll," the percentage is calculated on the electoral roll in accordance with article 3. Voting within the Legislative Branch is governed by the specific rule established in article 23.

---

### Article 3: Constitutional Subjects

This Constitution recognizes three categories of subjects:

1. Person: Every human being present in the national territory.
2. Citizen: A person who holds nationality in accordance with the law.
3. Citizen with the right to vote: A citizen who meets the conditions established in article 4.

Unless explicitly restricted by this Constitution or by law in conformity with it, every person enjoys the rights and protections established for persons. Every citizen additionally enjoys those established for citizens. Every citizen with the right to vote additionally enjoys those established for citizens with the right to vote.

Citizens retain all rights established for persons regardless of their geographic location.

When this Constitution says "the people," it refers to the body of citizens.

When this Constitution says "electoral roll," it refers to the register of citizens with the right to vote. The scope of the electoral roll, whether national or district-level, is specified in each article.

Restrictions on rights arising from a final criminal conviction are governed by law, within the limits of this Constitution.

---

### Article 4: Universal Suffrage

Every citizen who reaches the voting age has the right to suffrage. Each citizen has one vote of equal value: one citizen, one vote. Suffrage may not be restricted except within the age limits established in this article.

The voting age is 18 years by default. This value may be adjusted between 18 and 23 years by Level N5 consensus of the Legislative Branch. The limits of this range may only be modified through the reform procedure established in article 67.

---

### Article 5: Secrecy and Verifiability of the Vote

The vote is secret by default, except for votes of the Legislative Branch, which are public in accordance with article 23. No person, other than the citizen who cast it, may know the direction of the vote. The vote is non-delegable: it may not be sold, delegated, or transferred. The vote is verifiable: each voter may verify that their vote was counted correctly. The electoral process must be auditable by any citizen with the right to vote.

The specific technological and organizational details for implementing these guarantees are defined during the Bootstrap Process in accordance with Title III.

If a voting process has failed to comply with the guarantees established in this article, any citizen with the right to vote may petition for its nullification before the Judicial Branch. If the number of affected votes is sufficient to have altered the outcome, the judge shall declare the vote null and it must be repeated. Those responsible shall in all cases be subject to the criminal consequences established by ordinary legislation.

---

### Article 6: Guarantee of Popular Annulment

This Constitution permanently guarantees the right to create mechanisms for the direct popular annulment of legislative decisions. No law, regulation, or decision of any branch of government may prohibit the creation or operation of such mechanisms.

If a popular annulment mechanism is implemented, the level of citizen support required to annul a decision shall correspond to the minimum consensus level required for that decision: Level N1 decisions may be annulled with the support of 51% of the national electoral roll, Level N2 decisions with 60%, and so forth in accordance with the consensus levels N1 through N6 established in article 2. As an exception, the legislation that develops the specific grounds for states of exception pursuant to article 56 may be annulled with the support of 51% of the national electoral roll, regardless of the legislative consensus level with which it was approved. Every popular annulment process must comply with the guarantees of secrecy, verifiability, and public audit established in articles 5 and 17.

---

### Article 7: Right to Territorial Self-Determination

It is prohibited to prevent by law any territory from exercising the right to self-determination. The process of territorial independence must be regulated by law, but under no circumstances may it be prohibited or suppressed.

The law may establish requirements and conditions for the independence process. These requirements may be demanding, but shall be limited by the following conditions: the minimum duration of an independence process, from its formal declaration to its definitive resolution, whether approval or rejection, is 21 years; the maximum duration is 42 years.

---

### Article 8: Equality Before the Law and Public Accountability

No member of the branches of government enjoys any immunity or legal privilege by reason of their office. All are citizens subject to the same laws as any other citizen. Criminal prosecution of any public officeholder does not require prior authorization from the branch to which they belong or from any other body.

Criminal proceedings against public officeholders may be processed on a priority basis over proceedings against ordinary citizens. This Constitution does not establish the method of prioritization, but guarantees that such a mechanism may exist and shall not be suppressed by ordinary law.

Any person who loses a public office due to a final criminal conviction is permanently disqualified from holding any position in the public administration, regardless of the branch to which the office belonged. Modification of this disqualification requires Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 9: Official Language

The State has at least one official language. The official language is the language in which laws are drafted, judicial proceedings are conducted, public administration is exercised, and compulsory education is guaranteed. In case of conflict of interpretation between linguistic versions of a legal provision, the version in the official language prevails.

By default, the official language is the one that held such status under the regime prior to the entry into force of this Constitution. During the Bootstrap Process in accordance with Title III, the Legislative Branch may confirm or modify the official language by Level N4 consensus.

The designation of an official language does not prohibit the use of other languages in the private sphere or in public life. The State may recognize co-official languages in specific territories through ordinary legislation, provided that the official language retains its mandatory character in the domains established in this article.

# TITLE II: ELECTORAL SYSTEM

### Article 10: Representation by Territorial Districts

Representatives of the Legislative Branch are elected by territorial districts through single-member elections. Each district elects one representative.

Districts may have a system of alternates to ensure continuity of representation, with a minimum of 0 and a maximum of 2 alternates per district. When a district is left without a representative or available alternates, a new election is called in accordance with article 13.

The number of alternates per district may be modified by Level N4 consensus, either at the national level (a Legislative decision applicable to all districts) or at the district level (a district decision applicable only to that district), according to procedures established by ordinary law.

Popular power at the district level prevails over the national Legislature only in the cases explicitly established in this Constitution. Outside of these cases, district-level popular power may not override decisions of the national Legislature.

---

### Article 11: Revocability of Representatives

Representatives of the Legislative Branch shall always be directly revocable by the people of the district that elected them. This mechanism applies exclusively to legislative representatives in their electoral district. No law, regulation, or decision of any branch of government may suppress this right.

---

### Article 12: Recall Procedure

This article establishes the default procedure for the recall of Legislative Branch representatives in accordance with article 11.

Initiative:
Any citizen with the right to vote, individually or in a group, may initiate a recall proceeding. This capacity for individual initiative may only be modified by Level N6 consensus.

Call:
Requires the collection of signatures from 10% of the district electoral roll within a maximum period of 31 days.

Vote:
Once called, the vote is open to every citizen with the right to vote registered in the district who has been registered in the district for at least 2 years. A minimum participation of 25% of the district electoral roll is required for the vote to be valid. The entire recall process, from the formal call for the vote to the result, may not exceed 2 weeks in duration.

Effect:
If the result reaches Level N4 consensus in favor of recall, the mandate is extinguished and a new election is called within a maximum of 7 days.

Reflection Period:
No new recall may be called against the successor before 4 months have elapsed since their election.

Configurability:
The numerical parameters of this article may be modified by Level N6 consensus. These are: 10% of the district electoral roll signatures required for the call, 31 days maximum for signature collection, Level N4 required for successful recall, 7 days maximum to call a new election after recall, 4 months reflection period before a new recall, 2 weeks maximum total process duration, 25% minimum participation of the district electoral roll, and 2 years minimum registered in the district to vote in a recall.

During the Bootstrap Process, these numerical parameters may be modified with Level N2 consensus, but the recall mechanism itself may not be eliminated. The details of the Bootstrap Process are established in Title III of this Constitution.

---

### Article 13: Two-Round Electoral System in Districts

This article applies to elections of Legislative Branch representatives by district.

In the first round, if a candidate reaches or exceeds 51% of valid votes, they are proclaimed elected directly. If no candidate exceeds 51%, a second round is held between the two candidates with the most votes within a maximum of 7 days. The candidate who obtains more votes in the second round is proclaimed elected.

A minimum participation of 25% of the district electoral roll is required for the election to be valid in each round. If this minimum is not reached in any round, the entire election is repeated from the first round within 7 days. This continues until the required minimum participation is reached.

The numerical parameters of this article may be modified by Level N6 consensus. These are: 51% of valid votes required in the first round, 25% minimum participation of the district electoral roll, 7 days maximum between the first and second round, and 7 days maximum to repeat the election if minimum participation is not reached.

---

### Article 14: District Size

The size of each district is between 95,000 and 120,000 inhabitants. The exact figure is determined by the District Corruptibility Optimization Algorithm (DCOA), whose complete specifications are as follows:

Objective function: Maximize the minimum value of the 25th percentile of income across all districts in the country. That is, the optimal district distribution is the one in which the district with the lowest 25th percentile of income has that value as high as possible.

Input data:
1. Population data by the smallest available geographic unit, such as municipality, census tract, or equivalent.
2. Income distribution data by geographic unit.
3. Geographic map of the national territory.

Constraints:
1. Each district has between 95,000 and 120,000 inhabitants.
2. Geographic continuity: each district must be a continuous territory, without enclaves or exclaves.
3. Historical-cultural coherence: the delimitation respects provincial, municipal, or county boundaries. It may only cross these boundaries when strictly necessary to meet the population constraints established in this article.
4. Exception for geographic impossibility: when the constraints of geographic continuity and historical-cultural coherence prevent forming a district within the established population range, that district is permitted to exceed or fall short of the limits, provided the deviation is the minimum necessary.

The numerical parameters of this article may be modified by Level N6 consensus. These are: 95,000 and 120,000 inhabitants as the population range limits per district. During the Bootstrap Process, the Legislature may establish different parameters by Level N3 consensus.

---

### Article 15: Periodic District Update

Electoral districts are updated every 21 years. The update is carried out using the District Corruptibility Optimization Algorithm (DCOA) in accordance with article 14. The complete specifications of the DCOA are established in this Constitution and are public and auditable. The process is executed by an independent technical commission and ratified by the Legislative Branch by Level N1 consensus.

If the Legislature rejects the update, the commission may produce a new proposal with corrected or updated data. If the new proposal is also rejected, the current district distribution remains in effect until the next scheduled update.

The first district distribution shall be established during the Transition Process (article 18). The first update shall take place 21 years after the entry into force of this Constitution.

The district update period of 21 years may be modified by Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 16: Electoral Financing

Public financing:
If public financing for electoral campaigns exists, the amount must be identical for all candidates without exception. All public electoral financing is transparent and recorded in a publicly auditable system. Any citizen may verify the origin and destination of public electoral funds.

Private donations:
Private donations to candidates or campaigns are subject to a strict limit. Only citizens with the right to vote may make private donations; legal entities, such as corporations, associations, or foundations, are prohibited from donating to electoral campaigns. The maximum donation per citizen with the right to vote is 10% of the salary corresponding to the 25th percentile of income nationwide. Each citizen with the right to vote may make only 1 donation per electoral process.

The origin of private donations is secret. The destination and amount thereof are transparent and publicly auditable.

Protection against illegal financing:
If it is detected that the total amount raised by all candidates exceeds the legal maximum permitted, defined as the number of citizens with the right to vote multiplied by the maximum individual donation, the election shall be declared null. The funds shall be expropriated and returned to the donors. The surplus money shall be distributed equally among all candidates in the new elections, which shall begin within 7 days. If this situation recurs, the elections shall be held in the second call regardless of whether this situation occurs again. If those responsible for the illegal financing are identified, each culprit shall pay a penalty of 10 times the amount by which they exceeded the legal limit. Payment may be enforced by any means, including expropriation of assets.

Reflection period:
During the 7 days prior to any election, all forms of electoral campaigning are prohibited. During this period, candidates may only address the electorate through public events in previously agreed spaces, guaranteeing each candidate the same amount of time and conditions. Organizational details are established by electoral legislation. Violations are sanctioned in accordance with electoral legislation.

Configurability:
The numerical parameters of this article may be modified by Level N6 consensus. These are: 10% of the P25 as the maximum individual donation, 1 donation per citizen per electoral process, 7 days to call new elections after annulment, the limit on the number of calls before a definitive election, and 7 days of reflection period prior to the election.

---

### Article 17: Verifiable Remote Voting

Voting may be exercised remotely through a system that simultaneously guarantees:

1. Secrecy of the vote: impossibility of knowing who voted for what.
2. Individual verifiability: each voter may verify that their vote was counted.
3. Public audit: any citizen with the right to vote may verify the integrity of the process.

This Constitution does not prescribe a specific technology: what matters are the guarantees of the process, not the specific technical method. The system must comply with the guarantees established in article 5 regardless of the technology employed.

# TITLE III: TRANSITION PROCESS

### Article 18: Institutional Transition

The Transition Process begins immediately after the approval of this Constitution and ends when the President elected in accordance with article 24 takes office. During this period, the regime prior to the entry into force of this Constitution continues governing on an interim basis, exercising exclusively executive functions and subject to the principles and provisions of this Constitution.

The prior regime publishes the result of the District Corruptibility Optimization Algorithm in accordance with article 14, together with all the data used for its execution, so that any citizen may verify the result, within a maximum of 3 weeks from the approval of this Constitution. Following the publication and formation of districts, elections are called in the following order, each in a distinct and consecutive month: first, elections to the Legislative Branch in accordance with article 13; second, elections to the Executive Branch in accordance with article 24.

If a state of exception is declared during the Transition Process in accordance with article 55, the prior regime assumes control until the cessation of the state of exception, always under the principles of this Constitution. After cessation, the Transition Process resumes from the point at which it was interrupted. This rule applies recursively: if a new state of exception is declared during the resumed Transition Process, the same mechanism applies.

---

### Article 19: Bootstrap Process - Special Mandate

The Bootstrap Process is the 1-year period immediately following the completion of the Transition Process (article 18). This 1-year duration may not be modified during the Bootstrap Process itself. During this period, certain consensus levels required by this Constitution are reduced as specified in each corresponding article.

If a state of exception is declared during the Bootstrap Process in accordance with article 55, the Bootstrap Process is suspended for the duration of the state of exception. After cessation, the Bootstrap Process resumes with the remaining time that was left on the day prior to the declaration of the state of exception, with a minimum of 2 months. This rule applies recursively: if a new state of exception is declared during the resumed Bootstrap Process, the same mechanism applies.

Once the Bootstrap Process has ended, the consensus reductions applied during it are no longer in effect. From that point forward, the ordinary consensus levels established in each article apply. The Bootstrap Process may not be reactivated; it is a one-time mechanism applicable only during the initial establishment phase of this Constitution.

# TITLE IV: SEPARATION AND CHECKS OF THE BRANCHES OF GOVERNMENT

### Article 20: Separation of Powers

The Legislative, Executive, and Judicial branches shall be independent of one another. No branch may exercise functions constitutionally assigned to another. This separation is substantive, not merely formal: each branch must have independence of appointment and economic financing mechanisms that guarantee its effective autonomy.

No person may simultaneously hold more than one public office among those established in this Constitution. Acceptance of a new office entails automatic resignation from the prior one.

The financing of each branch must be as described in articles 30 through 32 inclusive and article 53 of this Constitution.

---

---

## SUBTITLE 1: LEGISLATIVE BRANCH

### Article 21: Legislative Branch - Composition

The Legislative Branch is composed of a single chamber of representatives elected by territorial districts in accordance with article 10. Representatives are elected for a term of 4 years and are directly revocable by the people of their district in accordance with articles 11 and 12.

---

### Article 22: Legislative Branch - Functions

The Legislative Branch shall exercise the monopoly of the legislative function except as otherwise provided in this Constitution, without prejudice to the constitutionality review exercised by the Judicial Branch in accordance with articles 42 and 43. Its functions include:

1. Approving, amending, or repealing laws.
2. Approving the annual State budget.
3. Approving or rejecting the budget proposed by the Judicial Branch in accordance with article 32.

---

### Article 23: Legislative Branch - Voting

All votes of the Legislature are public and recorded in a system auditable by any citizen. The vote of each representative must be known or verifiable by their constituents.

In votes of the Legislative Branch, consensus levels are calculated on the total number of seats. There is no abstention or blank vote: a representative who does not cast a vote in favor is counted as a vote against. Vacant seats are likewise counted as votes against.

The majority required by default to approve, amend, or repeal laws is Level N1 consensus, except in cases where this Constitution establishes specific qualified majorities.

The Legislature may not approve more than 7 laws per week.

---

---

## SUBTITLE 2: EXECUTIVE BRANCH

### Article 24: Executive Branch - President

The President of the Republic is elected by direct suffrage of all citizens with the right to vote in accordance with article 4, through a two-round system.

In the first round, if a candidate reaches or exceeds 51% of valid votes, they are proclaimed elected directly. If no candidate exceeds 51%, a second round is held between the two candidates with the highest percentage of votes. The candidate who obtains the highest percentage in the second round is proclaimed elected.

A minimum participation of 30% of the national electoral roll is required for the presidential election to be valid. If this minimum is not reached, the election is repeated within 14 days. This continues until the required minimum participation is reached. During this period, the outgoing President continues in office on an interim basis. If the outgoing President is unable to serve, the line of succession established in article 26 applies.

The presidential term is 5 years. No person may serve as President for more than two consecutive terms. After two consecutive terms, at least one full presidential term must elapse before the person may be elected again.

The numerical parameters of this article may be modified by Level N6 consensus. These are: 51% of valid votes required in the first round, 30% minimum participation of the national electoral roll, 14 days maximum to repeat the election if minimum participation is not reached, 5-year term, maximum of 2 consecutive terms, and 1-term waiting period before eligibility for re-election.

---

### Article 25: Executive Branch - Presidential Functions

The President exercises the entirety of the Executive Branch. Presidential functions include:

1. Head of State.
2. National defense and command of the Armed Forces (article 49).
3. Foreign relations and international representation.
4. Declaration of states of exception (article 55).
5. Domestic policy and governmental coordination.
6. Management of public expenditure in accordance with the budget approved by the Legislature.
7. Structure and direction of ministries.
8. Declaration of war and military decisions established in articles 50, 51, and 52.

The President may delegate the preparation and proposal of decisions to ministers appointed in accordance with article 29. Every decision of the Executive Branch requires the President's approval for its execution.

---

### Article 26: Presidential Succession

The President appoints a Vice President. The Vice President's term coincides with that of the President.

If the President dies, resigns, or is declared incapacitated in accordance with article 27, the Vice President assumes the Presidency on an interim basis. New presidential elections are called within a maximum of 45 days from the interim assumption, except during states of exception in accordance with article 61. The interim Vice President may run as a candidate. Whoever assumes the interim Presidency is subject to the same responsibilities, obligations, and consequences as the elected President. If the elections are not held within the established timeframe, whoever holds the interim Presidency is considered a usurper of the office, with all applicable legal consequences.

If the President is removed through the mutual destruction mechanism in accordance with article 39, the Vice President assumes the Presidency on an interim basis until new elections are held. During this period, the interim Vice President exercises exclusively institutional maintenance functions: they may not approve new budgets, appoint or dismiss ministers, or initiate new policies.

If the Vice President is unable to assume the interim Presidency, it is assumed by the longest-serving representative of the Legislature, under the same conditions. The Legislative Branch may extend the line of succession but not reduce it by Level N3 consensus; during the Bootstrap Period, by Level N1 consensus.

---

### Article 27: Presidential Incapacity

Presidential incapacity is declared exclusively by the Judicial Branch, upon request of the Vice President or the Legislative Branch by N2 consensus. The declaration is appealable by any citizen with the right to vote. If the declaration of incapacity is reversed on appeal, the responsible judge is subject to criminal sanction in accordance with article 45, of similar severity to those established for cases of unconstitutionality in article 44.

Grounds for incapacity include: coma and severe cognitive deterioration. The Legislative Branch may expand this list but not reduce it by N3 consensus; during the Bootstrap Period, by N2 consensus.

If the President recovers capacity before new presidential elections are held, they automatically resume office.

---

### Article 28: Ministries - Constitutional Limit

The maximum number of ministries by default is 20. The ministerial structure is the exclusive competence of the President in accordance with article 25. This Constitution does not establish a closed list of ministries, only their maximum number.

During the Bootstrap Process, this limit may be modified with Level N1 consensus.

---

### Article 29: Ministries - Appointment

Ministers are appointed and dismissed exclusively by the President, unless a minister is convicted by a final criminal judgment whose sentence includes removal from office. In that case, removal is automatic and independent of the President's will. Permanent disqualification is governed by article 8.

---

### Article 30: Prior Budget by Default

If the Legislature does not approve a new budget before the start of the fiscal year, the prior year's budget is automatically extended, adjusted for inflation over the period.

---

### Article 31: Executive Budget Limit

The Executive Branch may not commit expenditures exceeding the limits approved by the Legislature in the annual budget. All public expenditure whose amount per transaction exceeds the threshold established by law is recorded in a publicly auditable system accessible to any citizen or person with tax residency in the country, on a permanent basis.

---

---

## SUBTITLE 3: JUDICIAL BRANCH

### Article 32: Judicial Branch - Independence and Financing

The Judicial Branch is independent of the Legislative and Executive branches. Judges do not receive orders or instructions from any other branch.

The Supreme Court prepares and proposes the annual budget of the Judicial Branch. Any justice may submit a budget proposal. The proposal requires the approval of at least 2/3 of the justices of the Supreme Court to be presented to the Legislature. If this agreement is not reached, the prior year's budget is automatically maintained, adjusted for inflation. The Legislature may approve or reject the proposed budget by Level N3 consensus, but may not modify it. If the proposed budget is rejected, the prior year's budget is automatically maintained, adjusted for inflation.

The consensus level required for the rejection of the judicial budget may be modified by Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 33: Judicial Branch - Hierarchy

The judicial structure is organized in three levels:

1. First Instance: Ordinary courts.
2. Second Instance: Courts of appeal and cassation.
3. Supreme Court: Court of last resort.

The decisions of the Supreme Court are final in the judicial order, without prejudice to the popular annulment established in article 46.

Modification of the three-level structure requires Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 34: Right of Appeal and Cassation

Every party in a judicial proceeding has the right to at least one appeal and one cassation before a court of the immediately superior level. This right is exercised per case and per level: when multiple parties request review of the same case at the same level, the case ascends only once, not once for each party requesting it.

Appeal allows full review of facts and law. Cassation is an additional review that examines exclusively the correct application of law, without re-examination of facts except in cases of manifest arbitrariness in their assessment.

When this Constitution establishes that a decision of the Supreme Court is subject to appeal, the appeal is resolved by a different justice of the same Court. The justice who issued the appealed decision may not participate in resolving the appeal. The assigned justice is selected through a deterministic random mechanism. The default procedure is as follows: a cryptographic hash function is applied to the biometric data of the appellant. The biometric datum is selected according to the following priority: fingerprint of the left hand (from little finger to thumb), of the right hand (from thumb to little finger), of the left foot (from little toe to big toe), of the right foot (from big toe to little toe), retina of the left eye, retina of the right eye. The first available datum is used. If none of these biometric data are available, those of the justice who issued the appealed decision are used. The justices are ordered by seniority in office, with the most senior being first. The result of applying the modulo operation of the number of justices to the hash result determines the index of the assigned justice according to the established seniority order. If the result points to the justice who issued the appealed decision, it is incremented by one and the modulo is applied again. The assignment method may be modified during the Bootstrap Process by Level N1 consensus, provided the alternative method is deterministic, non-manipulable, and publicly verifiable.

The total number of appeals and cassations in a single case may not exceed 5.

---

### Article 35: Appointment of Judges - Principle

Judges are appointed through a procedure that guarantees judicial independence. Judicial appointment requires:

1. A law degree accredited by the Judicial Branch.
2. Passing a public qualification examination.

The qualification examination is designed, administered, and graded exclusively by the Judicial Branch. No other branch, body, or external entity participates in its preparation or evaluation.

Valid degrees are determined by agreement of at least 2/3 of the justices of the Supreme Court. This determination may only be modified once every 8 years. Every modification includes a 1-year transition period during which previously accredited degrees retain their validity.

The same accreditation regime applies to law schools and legal institutions recognized by the Judicial Branch. During the Bootstrap Process, all law schools existing under the prior regime are accredited by default. After the appointment of the first cohort of Supreme Court justices, the initial list may be modified by Level N1 consensus of the justices. Thereafter, every modification requires 2/3 agreement in accordance with the general regime of this article.

Modification of these requirements requires Level N5 consensus of the Legislative Branch.

---

### Article 36: Supreme Court - Composition

The Supreme Court is composed of 9 justices. Each justice is appointed for a maximum term of 12 years. The term is renewable after an exclusion period of 8 years from the completion of the prior term. Renewal is staggered: every 4 years, 3 justices are renewed. The initial constitution and renewal of the Supreme Court are governed by the procedures established in article 38.

The numerical parameters of this article (9 justices, 12-year term, 8-year exclusion, 4-year renewal cycle, 3 justices per cycle) may be modified by Level N5 consensus. Any modification of these parameters must maintain the coherence of the staggered renewal: the total number of justices must be divisible by the number of justices renewed per cycle. Modification or elimination of this coherence requirement requires Level N6 consensus.

---

### Article 37: Supreme Court - Appointment

Candidates for Supreme Court justice are proposed exclusively by the following institutions, each with at least 10 years of operation in the country:

1. Bar associations.
2. Law schools accredited in accordance with article 35.
3. Associations of judges from courts below the Supreme Court.

Candidates for Supreme Court justice must demonstrate a minimum of 5 years of experience as a judge. This minimum may be adjusted between 5 and 10 years; during the Bootstrap Process by Level N1 consensus, outside of it by Level N4 consensus.

The final election is conducted through direct and binding vote of legal professionals. Each voter has one vote of equal value; the vote is non-delegable. The definition of who qualifies as a legal professional for the purposes of this vote is established during the Bootstrap Process by Level N1 consensus. The total number of candidates must be strictly greater than the number of vacancies. The specific procedure, including the number of candidates proposed by each institution and the voting system, is established during the Bootstrap Process by Level N2 consensus. Modification of any parameter of this article outside the Bootstrap Process requires Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 38: Supreme Court - Procedures for Constitution and Renewal

Initial constitution:

During the Bootstrap Process, the 9 justices are appointed simultaneously in accordance with article 37. To establish the staggered renewal, differentiated initial terms are assigned by verifiable random drawing. The drawing must be deterministic, non-manipulable, and publicly verifiable. The specific drawing procedure is established during the Bootstrap Process by Level N1 consensus, provided it meets these three characteristics. Outside the Bootstrap Process, this procedure may not be modified.

If no alternative procedure is established during the Bootstrap Process, the default procedure is as follows: a cryptographic hash function (such as SHA-256 or Keccak-256) is applied to the biometric data of each justice. The biometric datum is selected according to the following priority: fingerprint of the left hand (from little finger to thumb), of the right hand (from thumb to little finger), of the left foot (from little toe to big toe), of the right foot (from big toe to little toe), retina of the left eye, retina of the right eye. The first available datum is used. The 3 justices with the highest value in the first 2 bytes of the hash serve an initial term of 4 years, the 3 with the highest value among the remaining 6 serve 8 years, and the remaining 3 serve the full term of 12 years. Thereafter, all terms are 12 years.

In case of a tie in the first 2 bytes of the hash between two or more justices contending for the same term bracket, successive subsequent bytes of the hash (third, fourth, etc.) are compared until the tie is resolved. The justice with the highest value in the tiebreaking byte receives the shorter term in the contested bracket.

Staggered renewal:

Every 4 years, 3 new justices are appointed in accordance with article 37, replacing the 3 justices whose terms have expired. Outgoing justices may be proposed again as candidates once the exclusion period established in article 36 has elapsed.

Early vacancies:

In case of a vacancy before the expiration of the term, the replacement is appointed in accordance with article 37 and serves only the remaining time of the original term, preserving the staggered renewal calendar. While the vacancy is not filled, the vacant seat is counted as a vote against in Supreme Court decisions that require consensus among justices.

## SUBTITLE 4: BALANCE BETWEEN BRANCHES

### Article 39: Mutual Destruction - Mechanism

The Legislature may remove the President by Level N1 consensus vote. The President may dissolve the Legislature. Both actions automatically entail the calling of simultaneous new elections for the Legislature (article 13) and the President (article 24) within a minimum of 7 days and a maximum of 30 days.

The numerical parameters of this article may be modified by Level N6 consensus. They are not modifiable during the Bootstrap Process, except for the timeframe for calling new elections, which may be set within the range of 7 to 30 days by Level N1 consensus. Outside the Bootstrap Process, the timeframe may be adjusted within this range by Level N4 consensus.

The mutual destruction mechanism may not be activated during a state of exception declared in accordance with article 55. If a state of exception occurs during the period between the activation of mutual destruction and the holding of new elections, the dissolved Legislature is reconvened exclusively to exercise the oversight functions established in articles 58 through 63 inclusive. Outside these functions, the Legislature remains dissolved.

---

### Article 40: Mutual Destruction - Deactivation Period

Following the activation of the mutual destruction mechanism (article 39), an asymmetric deactivation period operates during which neither party may activate the mutual destruction mechanism. The deactivation period applies differently to the party that invoked it and the party that received it:

If activated by the Legislature:
- Deactivation period for the Legislature: 6 months from the new elections.
- Deactivation period for the Executive: 3 months from the new elections.

If activated by the President:
- Deactivation period for the Executive: 12 months from the new elections.
- Deactivation period for the Legislature: 3 months from the new elections.

The numerical parameters of this article (6 months, 12 months, 3 months) may be modified by Level N6 consensus.

---

### Article 41: Serious Breach by the President

When the President commits a serious breach of final judicial resolutions, a process may be initiated to call new presidential elections.

Initiative:
1. Legislative route: Level N1 consensus of the Legislature.
2. Popular route: a group of at least 10% of the national electoral roll.

Resolution:
The Supreme Court directly resolves the process and determines whether the breach constitutes any of the grounds established in this article. The decision of the Supreme Court is subject to a single appeal (article 34) by any citizen with the right to vote.

Grounds for serious breach:
1. Refusing to execute a final judgment of the Supreme Court.
2. Such other grounds as may be established by ordinary legislation.

Ground 1 may not be eliminated or modified. The list of additional grounds established by ordinary legislation may be expanded or contracted by Level N4 consensus.

The numerical parameters of this article (N1, 10%) may be modified by Level N6 consensus.

If the Supreme Court declares a serious breach by final resolution, new presidential elections are called in accordance with the deadlines and succession rules of article 26. The interim officeholder exercises exclusively the limited functions described in the second scenario of article 26.

## SUBTITLE 5: JUDICIAL LEGITIMACY REVIEW

### Article 42: Constitutional Supremacy

This Constitution is the supreme law of the legal order. Any law, regulation, administrative act, or judicial decision that contravenes this Constitution is null and void by operation of law. Constitutionality review is diffuse: any judge, at any level, may declare the inapplicability of a provision in the specific case if they consider it contrary to this Constitution. This declaration of inapplicability may be appealed in accordance with the layered system established in article 43.

---

### Article 43: Constitutionality Review Process

Any citizen with the right to vote may challenge the constitutionality of any enacted law before the courts, regardless of how much time has passed since its enactment. Each law may be submitted for constitutional review once every 6 years, counted from the filing of the challenge. Each citizen may file a challenge or a group of challenges once every 4 months. Challenges may be filed individually or in groups of up to 10 laws, with an independent verdict for each law in the group. Only one active constitutional review process per law may exist at any given time; if a package contains a law already under review, the package is admitted but that law is excluded. The maximum number of groups under simultaneous review is 10, which establishes a limit of 100 laws under simultaneous constitutional review. The challenge is filed before a court of first instance and resolved according to the following layered system:

1. First Instance: The court may declare inapplicable a provision it considers contrary to this Constitution. This declaration has effect only for the specific case. The court must provide a detailed statement of the reasons why it considers the provision to contravene the Constitution. Any citizen with the right to vote may appeal this decision to second instance within a maximum of 2 weeks from the first instance decision, regardless of their participation in the initial proceeding. Once this period expires without appeal, the first instance decision becomes final.

2. Second Instance (Cassation): The second instance court reviews exclusively the legal reasoning applied. It may confirm the inapplicability, revoke it, or qualify its terms. If it confirms the inapplicability, the provision becomes inapplicable for all cases. Any citizen with the right to vote may appeal this decision to the Supreme Court within a maximum of 2 weeks from the second instance decision. Once this period expires without appeal, the second instance decision becomes permanent.

3. Supreme Court: It is the ultimate interpreter of the constitutionality of laws in the judicial order. Its decisions are binding on all courts in the country. When it declares a provision unconstitutional, that provision becomes inapplicable for all cases. The decision of the Supreme Court may only be reversed through popular annulment in accordance with article 46 or through constitutional reform. The Legislature may enact a new law with substantive changes that addresses the constitutional problem identified.

The right of appeal is exercised in accordance with article 34: per case and per level. When multiple parties challenge the constitutionality of the same provision at the same level, the challenge filed first has priority; the others are incorporated into the proceeding already initiated. The case ascends only once, not once for each challenge.

The numerical parameters of this article (6 years between reviews, 10 laws per group, 10 simultaneous groups, 2 weeks appeal period, 4 months waiting period per citizen) may be modified by Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 44: Criminal Liability Arising from the Constitutionality Review Process

Criminal liability of legislators:
If a law is declared unconstitutional at any judicial level and the decision becomes final, the legislators who voted in favor of that law shall be subject to criminal sanction in accordance with article 45.

Criminal liability of judges for error in constitutionality review:
A judge who rules on the constitutionality of a law assumes personal responsibility for their decision, in accordance with the following rules:
1. Mechanism of demonstration: Error is demonstrated through the review procedure established in article 43, subject to its deadlines and limitations, applied to the constitutional text and configurations in effect at the time the decision was issued.
2. Window of liability: The judge is liable if within 10 years following their decision it is demonstrated that it was erroneous. A review proceeding initiated within this window is resolved even if the window has expired at the time of the judgment.
3. Severity: Sanctions shall be of similar or greater severity than those applicable to legislators, in accordance with article 45. For Supreme Court justices, the minimum sanction for a demonstrated error is 20 years of imprisonment. This sanction is constitutional and may be modified during the Bootstrap Process by Level N3 consensus. Outside the Bootstrap Process, its modification requires Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 45: Sanctions for Legislators and Judges

The specific sanctions for legislators and judges of courts below the Supreme Court are established during the Bootstrap Process by Level N3 consensus and must be deterrent. The default sanction for legislators is the payment of 10 years of their salary in effect at the time of the sanction, as a debt. Sanctions for ordinary popular annulment are established in the same manner but must be of lesser severity than those for unconstitutionality.

If the sanction is economic, the fine may only be paid through capital obtained from the personal labor earnings of the sanctioned individual; payment through investments, donations, sale of assets, or any other means other than personal labor is not permitted.

Subsequent modification of these sanctions requires Level N5 consensus.

---

## SUBTITLE 6: POPULAR ANNULMENT

### Article 46: Popular Annulment of Judicial Decisions

The people may annul any judicial decision that has become final, regardless of the level at which it acquired finality, through popular annulment in accordance with the hierarchy of legitimacy established in article 1.

Call:

1. Reflection period: At least 3 months must elapse since the judicial decision became final.
2. Scope and thresholds:
   a) Constitutional: Applies to judicial decisions on the constitutionality of laws. Initiating group: at least 10% of the national electoral roll. Signatures required: Level N1 of the national electoral roll.
   b) Ordinary: Applies to ordinary non-constitutional judicial decisions. Initiating group: at least 30% of the national electoral roll. Signatures required: of the national electoral roll, a consensus level equal to the highest level that was required to approve any of the laws on which the judicial decision was based, with a minimum of Level N1 in accordance with article 23.

Signature collection:

Signature collection must be formally declared before it begins. Only signatures collected after the declaration are counted; prior signatures are not valid. The maximum period to complete the collection is 2 years from the declaration. Only signatures of living citizens with the right to vote at the time of verification are counted; if a signatory dies, their signature ceases to be valid. Seven days after the corresponding signature threshold is reached, the judicial decision is annulled.

Effect:

When popular annulment reaches the established threshold:

1. The judicial decision is annulled.
2. In the constitutional domain: the provision that had been declared unconstitutional regains its force (or its nullity is confirmed, depending on the direction of the annulment).
3. Popular annulment does not constitute binding precedent: future disputes over similar provisions must be resolved again through the ordinary judicial process, unless a new popular annulment occurs.
4. The judge responsible for the annulled decision is subject to criminal sanction in accordance with article 45, of lesser severity than those established for cases of unconstitutionality in article 44.

The numerical parameters of this article (3 months of reflection, 10% and 30% of the national electoral roll, 7 days after reaching the threshold, 2 years to complete signature collection) may be modified by Level N6 consensus.

## SUBTITLE 7: FISCAL OVERSIGHT AND TRANSPARENCY

### Article 47: Budgetary Transparency

All public budgets are published in a publicly auditable system. Publication includes:

1. Approved budget disaggregated by line items.
2. Budget execution updated every 30 days.
3. Comparison between budgeted and executed amounts.
4. Justification of significant deviations.

Any citizen or person with tax residency in the country may access this information free of charge. Budgetary transparency admits no exceptions other than those expressly established in article 53.

---

### Article 48: Public Audit

Any citizen has the right to audit the public data recorded in the systems established in this Constitution. The State must provide an accessible, free, and comprehensible inquiry process. Within a maximum of 30 days after the inquiry, the requested information must have reached the citizen. Failure to meet this deadline shall always entitle the requester to seek judicial remedy before the Judicial Branch.

## SUBTITLE 8: ARMED FORCES

### Article 49: Commander in Chief

The President of the Republic is the Commander in Chief of the Armed Forces. The President exercises direct operational command of all military branches. This command is subject to the constitutional limits established in articles 50 through 54 inclusive.

---

### Article 50: Declaration of War

The declaration of war is proposed by the President and requires the approval of the Legislative Branch by Level N5 consensus. Immediate defense against attack or invasion does not require a prior declaration of war (article 51). The declaration of war may be revoked on the initiative of the President or the Legislative Branch, provided the national territory is not under attack. In both cases, revocation requires the approval of the Legislative Branch by Level N3 consensus.

---

### Article 51: Military Operations Without a Declaration of War

1. Defense of the national territory against an actual or imminent armed attack: The President may and must order immediate defense without prior legislative approval. The President must notify the Legislature immediately after ordering the defense. Ordering a defense when no actual attack exists constitutes an act of equal gravity to those described in article 52 and is punished in the same manner.

2. Rescue of citizens in a situation of imminent danger abroad: The President may order the operation without prior legislative approval. The President must notify the Legislature immediately after ordering the operation. If the operation extends beyond 30 days and involves a significant use of force, it requires legislative ratification by Level N3 consensus.

The numerical parameters of this article (30 days, N3) may be modified by Level N5 consensus. During the Bootstrap Process, by Level N3 consensus.

---

### Article 52: Accountability for Secret Operations

The President may order secret military operations without prior legislative approval. If such an operation results in:

1. Military disaster (significant loss of life or resources).
2. Initiation of an unauthorized armed conflict.

If a secret operation of this scope is discovered or announced, the Legislative Branch may, by Level N1 consensus, initiate judicial proceedings before the Supreme Court for the application of the following punishment: the President is held personally accountable through the amputation of one hand. The decision of the Supreme Court is appealable by any citizen with the right to vote in accordance with the ordinary appeal procedure established in article 34.

The default punishment may be modified during the Bootstrap Process by Level N3 consensus, provided the alternative punishment is of a personal and physical nature. The numerical parameters of this article (N1) may be modified by Level N5 consensus.

---

### Article 53: Military Budget

The budget of the Armed Forces is approved annually by the Legislature through the ordinary budget approval procedure. All military expenditure is recorded in a publicly auditable system, allowing permanent citizen audit.

Exceptions:

1. Intelligence items and classified operations: They are recorded in aggregate form (without operational detail) and are audited by a specific legislative commission with classified access.
2. Sensitive equipment: The specific location and capabilities may be classified, but total costs must be public.

---

### Article 54: Separation of Forces

The Armed Forces are organized into separate branches:

1. Army: Land defense.
2. Navy: Naval and coastal defense.
3. Air Force: Air and space defense.

The chiefs of each branch are appointed and removed by the President. The President may not appoint themselves as branch chief. After removing a branch chief, the President must appoint a replacement. The replacement may not be removed until 1 month has elapsed since their appointment, except during a state of exception. The replacement may not be the person removed, nor a relative up to the second degree of consanguinity or affinity of the President, nor a person currently serving as Vice President or minister. The Legislature may revoke the appointment of any military chief by Level N4 consensus.

The Legislature may create new branches by Level N3 consensus. The three branches established in this article may not be eliminated or merged; modification of this protection requires Level N5 consensus.

## SUBTITLE 9: STATES OF EXCEPTION

### Article 55: States of Exception - Principle

States of exception may only be declared, in accordance with article 56, in the face of serious crises that cannot be managed through ordinary means. Declaring a state of exception without a genuine crisis constitutes a serious crime, punishable by default with 10 years of imprisonment. This parameter may be modified during the Bootstrap Process by Level N2 consensus and subsequently by Level N3 consensus. By default, there are three types of states of exception, established in article 56. Every state of exception is subject to the six mandatory limits established in articles 58 through 63 inclusive. No crisis, however grave, authorizes the suspension of these six limits.

---

### Article 56: Types of States of Exception

Three types of states of exception are established, classified according to the scope of the crisis. Each type specifies the rights that may be temporarily restricted within the affected zone. Outside of the rights expressly mentioned, all others remain in force. All exceptional powers cease automatically upon the termination of the state of exception, restoring all conditions to the prior state. In all cases, they are subject to the six limits of articles 58 through 63 inclusive.

1. Natural and Technological Scope:
- Causes: Natural catastrophe, health crisis, technological disaster (nuclear accident, power outage of more than 48 hours in duration, toxic spill, or other events of equivalent severity).
- Declaration: Level N1 consensus of the Legislature, with absolute priority on the agenda. In case of imminent danger to life, it is activated automatically.
- Restrictable rights: Defined in the Bootstrap Process.
- Temporary powers: Defined in the Bootstrap Process.

2. Domestic Security Scope:
- Causes: Multiple terrorist attacks, attempted coup d'etat.
- Declaration: Automatic activation. Legislative ratification within 7 days with N2 consensus; without ratification, it is automatically revoked.
- Restrictable rights (exclusively for suspects): Defined in the Bootstrap Process.
- Temporary powers: Defined in the Bootstrap Process.

3. War Scope:
- Causes: Declared war, foreign invasion, armed insurgency.
- Declaration: Automatic activation without prior legislative approval.
- Restrictable rights of attackers of the nation: All fundamental rights.
- Temporary powers: Transfer of civilian functions to military authority in the affected zone, plus those defined in the Bootstrap Process.

---

### Article 57: Configurability of States of Exception

All variables of article 56 are defined during the Bootstrap Process (Title III) with N1 consensus, except those explicitly excluded.

After the Bootstrap Process: modification of restrictable rights requires Level N4 consensus; modification of causes, declaration, temporary powers, and the creation of new types of states of exception requires Level N5 consensus. Legislation that develops the specific grounds for each cause requires Level N3 consensus to be enacted and is subject to the popular annulment exception established in article 6.

The restriction of all fundamental rights of attackers of the nation (War Scope) is not configurable during the Bootstrap Process; its subsequent modification requires Level N6 consensus. The transfer of civilian functions to military authority (War Scope) is not configurable during the Bootstrap Process; its subsequent modification requires Level N6 consensus.

---

### Article 58: Limit 1 - Temporal Limit

By default, the state of exception ends when the condition that gave rise to it disappears. If the condition disappears, the state of exception ceases automatically and immediately, without the need for a legislative vote.

If the condition persists, the Legislature must mandatorily pronounce on the continuation of the state of exception at 6 months from its activation. Thereafter, renewal follows a scheme of increasing consensus and decreasing intervals:

- First renewal (at 6 months): Level N1 consensus. Next review in 2 months.
- Second renewal: Level N2 consensus. Next review in 1 month.
- Third renewal: Level N3 consensus. Next review in 1 month.
- Fourth renewal: Level N4 consensus. Next review in 1 month.
- Fifth renewal and subsequent: Level N5 consensus. Review every 2 weeks.

If in any renewal the required consensus is not reached, the state of exception ceases automatically.

---

### Article 59: Limit 2 - Territorial Limit

The state of exception may only be applied in the geographic zone directly affected by the crisis that gives rise to it. The rest of the national territory maintains full constitutional normality. If the crisis is national in scope, the declaration of the state of exception applies to the entire national territory.

---

### Article 60: Limit 3 - Material Limit and Prohibition of Decrees

Exceptional powers are limited to a closed list specific to each type of state of exception in accordance with article 56. This list is established and modified in accordance with the consensus levels of article 57. Any measure that exceeds the powers established in the closed list is null and void by operation of law and constitutes abuse of authority. In case of abuse of authority, the Legislature may revoke the state of exception at any time by Level N1 consensus.

The Executive Branch may issue decrees exclusively during a state of exception declared in accordance with article 55. Outside of a state of exception, decrees are absolutely prohibited. Decrees issued during a state of exception are strictly temporary, cease automatically upon the termination of the state of exception, and under no circumstances may they annul or modify any article of this Constitution.

---

### Article 61: Limit 4 - Parliamentary Oversight

During any state of exception, the Legislative Branch functions normally. The mutual destruction mechanism (articles 39 and 40) is suspended during the state of exception. Scheduled elections are postponed only in case of objective physical impossibility in accordance with article 63. This suspension also applies to the 45-day deadline for new presidential elections established in article 26 (presidential succession); while the suspension lasts, the usurpation penalty of article 26 is not applicable. The Legislative Branch determines by Level N1 consensus when objective physical impossibility has ceased. Once determined, elections are held within a maximum of 45 days in accordance with article 26 and the usurpation penalty is reactivated.

---

### Article 62: Limit 5 - Judicial Oversight

The Judicial Branch maintains all its functions during states of exception. In particular:

1. It may review the legality of any measure adopted during the exception, including the legality and constitutionality of the declaration of the state of exception itself.
2. Any person whose rights applicable in the context of the state of exception (in accordance with article 56) are violated may petition for judicial proceedings.
3. Any citizen with the right to vote may petition a first instance judge for review of the legality and constitutionality of the declaration of the state of exception.

---

### Article 63: Limit 6 - Absolute Prohibitions

During any state of exception, the following are absolutely prohibited:

1. Modifying this Constitution through the reform procedures (articles 67 through 69 inclusive).
2. Modifying electoral law or the rules governing the conduct of elections.
3. Postponing elections except in cases of objective physical impossibility (e.g., active war zone, destruction of electoral infrastructure).
4. Postponing the commencement of judicial proceedings except in cases of objective physical impossibility (e.g., active war zone, destruction of judicial infrastructure).

Any act that violates these prohibitions is null and void by operation of law, without the need for a judicial declaration.

---

### Article 64: Post-Exception Accountability

Following the termination of any state of exception:

1. The Executive must submit a public report within a maximum of 60 days, detailing all measures adopted, their justification, results, and costs. Any citizen may access this report.
2. If a citizen reports irregularities, a first instance judge must order an independent audit of the measures adopted.
3. Officials who have abused exceptional powers or violated the six limits (articles 58 through 63 inclusive) may be criminally prosecuted. The state of exception does not exempt from liability for abuses.

Failure to submit the report within the deadline constitutes in itself an irregularity that must be prosecuted.

---

### Article 65: Automatic Termination of the State of Exception

The state of exception terminates automatically when any of the following circumstances occurs:

1. Disappearance of the condition that gave rise to it (article 58).
2. Non-renewal by the Legislature at any of the mandatory reviews (article 58).
3. Revocation by the Legislature in case of abuse of authority (article 60).
4. A final judicial declaration that the state of exception was declared without a genuine crisis in accordance with article 55.

Termination does not require any additional affirmative act: it operates automatically. Following termination, all exceptional measures cease immediately and full constitutional normality is restored.

---

# TITLE V: CONSTITUTIONAL REFORM AND PROTECTION

### Article 66: Entrenched Clauses - Inviolable Core

The following constitutional principles are entrenched clauses: they may not be eliminated through the ordinary reform procedure established in articles 68 and 69. They may only be modified through the special procedure established in article 67:

1. Popular Sovereignty (article 1): Power resides in the people through the mechanisms of direct single-member representation in small districts and revocability of the mandate of the individual to whom power has been delegated.
2. Hierarchy of Democratic Legitimacy (article 1): The people may oppose decisions of any delegated power in accordance with the procedures established in this Constitution. The delegated powers are equal among themselves and subject to reciprocal oversight.
3. Universal Suffrage (article 4): The right to vote of every citizen of legal age.
4. Voting Guarantees (article 5): The vote is secret, non-delegable, verifiable, and auditable. None of these properties may be eliminated except through this procedure.
5. Guarantee of Popular Annulment (article 6): The right to create mechanisms for the direct popular annulment of legislative decisions may not be suppressed.
6. Equality Before the Law (article 8): No member of the branches of government enjoys any immunity or legal privilege by reason of their office.
7. Official Language (article 9): The State must have at least one official language with mandatory status in legislation, justice, administration, and education.
8. Separation of Powers (article 20): Independence of the Legislative, Executive, and Judicial branches.
9. Balance of Powers (article 39): The mutual destruction mechanism between the Legislature and the Executive may not be eliminated.
10. Constitutional Supremacy (article 42): This Constitution is the supreme law of the legal order.
11. Right and Procedures for Constitutional Reform (articles 67 through 69 inclusive): The people always retain the right to reform this Constitution through the established procedures. The reform procedures may not be suppressed.
12. Fundamental Rights of the Democratic System (article 71): The rights established as necessary conditions for the functioning of democracy may not be eliminated.

---

### Article 67: Procedure for Reform of Entrenched Clauses

The reform of any of the entrenched clauses established in article 66 requires approval by popular referendum with Level N5 consensus of valid favorable votes and minimum participation of 75% of the national electoral roll. The referendum may be called by two routes:

1. Legislative route: Approval by Level N6 consensus of the Legislative Branch.
2. Popular route: Initiative of Level N1 of the citizens with the right to vote.

Double vote:

The reform requires two separate votes:

1. First vote: Approval of the reform proposal with the majorities established in this article.
2. Reflection period: A mandatory period of 2 years from the first vote.
3. Second vote: Confirmation of the reform with the same majorities.

If the second vote does not reach the required majorities, the reform proposal lapses and may not be resubmitted until the waiting period has elapsed.

Waiting period:

Once the procedure for reform of an entrenched clause has been completed, regardless of whether the reform is approved or rejected, that same clause may not be submitted to a new reform procedure until 21 years have elapsed since the last vote of the procedure.

Protection of parameters:

The parameters of this article may be modified through the procedures established in this same article.

---

### Article 68: Right of Ordinary Reform

Every part of this Constitution, except the entrenched clauses established in article 66, may be reformed through the following procedure:

1. Legislative route: Approval by Level N4 consensus of the Legislative Branch.
2. Popular route: Approval by referendum with Level N1 consensus of valid votes and minimum participation of 50% of the national electoral roll.

When an article contains parameters with a specific level of protection above N4, the reform of the article as a whole requires a consensus equal to or greater than the highest level of protection of the parameters it contains.

Ordinary reform enters into force 1 week after approval.

---

### Article 69: Initiative for Ordinary Reform

The initiative to propose ordinary reforms in accordance with article 68 may come from:

1. Legislative initiative: 33% of the members of the Legislative Branch.
2. Popular initiative: 10% of the national electoral roll through a petition in a publicly auditable system.

Both routes of initiative require final approval in accordance with the majorities established in article 68.

---

### Article 70: Supremacy of Constitutional Mechanisms

No fundamental right, whether essential (article 71) or complementary (article 72), may be invoked to block, annul, or circumvent the mechanisms, conditions, and limits established in this Constitution.

# TITLE VI: RIGHTS AND PROTECTIONS

### Article 71: Fundamental Rights of the Democratic System

The following rights are necessary conditions for the functioning of the democratic mechanisms established in this Constitution. These rights apply insofar as they guarantee the functioning of said mechanisms, and under no circumstances may they be used to alter, block, annul, or circumvent the mechanisms, conditions, and limits established in this Constitution as described in article 70.

1. Political expression: Every person has the right to express, disseminate, and debate political ideas without censorship.

2. Information: Every person has the right to access information and to disseminate it.

3. Political assembly and association: Every person has the right to assemble and associate peacefully for political purposes, including the organization necessary to exercise the mechanisms of recall (articles 11 and 12), popular annulment (article 46), and reform initiative (article 69).

4. Personal integrity: No person may be subjected to physical harm by reason of their political activity or their exercise of the vote.

5. Privacy of the vote and deliberation: Every person has the right to have their vote and their process of political deliberation remain private.

---

### Article 72: Complementary Fundamental Rights

During the Bootstrap Process in accordance with Title III, the people shall define by Level N2 consensus the catalog of fundamental rights complementary to those established in article 71. This catalog may include, among others: the right to general privacy and solitude, to property, to the trade of goods and services, to the practice of religion, to recreational and leisure activities, to reproduction and family life, to work and profession, to education, to private communication, to state protection, to freedom of movement, and to life.

Once adopted, the addition, modification, or elimination of any right from the catalog requires Level N5 consensus.

---

**End of Title VI**
